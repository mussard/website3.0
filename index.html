Multi-reference Perturbation Theory

I am currently involved in implementing a fully internally contracted version of multi-reference perturbation theories (NEVPT2/MRLCC) in a program interfaced with PySCF and Dice to calculate out-of-active space dynamical correlation on top of a SHCI calculation. The use of both the density fitting approximation for the integrals and the cumulant approximation for the high-order reduced density matrix involved in the equations will allow the treatment of larger systems of interest.

Spin-orbit coupling

We extended the heat bath configuration interaction (HCI) algorithm and its semistochastic version to treat relativistic effects and electron correlation on an equal footing in large active spaces. This work demonstrates that despite a significant increase in the size of the Hilbert space due to spin symmetry breaking by the spin-orbit coupling terms, HCI retains the ability to discard large parts of the low importance Hilbert space to deliver converged absolute and relative energies. For instance, by using just over 10^7 determinants we get converged excitation energies for Au atom in an active space containing (150o,25e) which has over 10^30 determinants.The relative accuracy of the different BP and X2C Hamiltonians are also compared on systems that range in atomic number from first row atoms to actinides.

Heat-bath Configuration Interaction

The recently-developed heat-bath configuration interaction (HCI) algorithm is used as an efficient active-space solver to perform multi-configuration self-consistent field calculations (HCISCF) with large active spaces. The converged active space orbitals obtained from HCISCF are found to be relatively insensitive to the accuracy of the HCI calculation, which allows to obtain nearly converged CASSCF energies with an estimated error of less than 1 mHa using the orbitals obtained from the HCISCF procedure in which the integral transformation is the dominant cost. For example, an HCISCF calculation on Fe-Porphyrin with an active space of (44e, 44o) took only 412 seconds per iteration on a single node containing 28 cores, out of which 185 seconds were spent in the HCI calculation and the remaining 227 seconds were mainly used for integral transformation, and found the correct spin symmetry for the gound state. Finally, since HCI is not invariant to unitary transformations within the active space, the active-space orbitals can be optimized using HCISCF to substantially speed up the convergence of the HCI energy to the Full CI limit.

Linear-response VMC

We extented variational Monte Carlo (VMC) to the calculation of electronic excitation energies and oscillator strengths using time-dependent linear-response theory. This makes use of the simple observation that the linear method for wave-function optimisation and the generalised eigenvalue equation of linear-response theory show analogous equations, allowing an easy formulation of the equations of linear-response VMC (LR-VMC) that involve the first- and second-order derivatives of the wave function with respect to the parameters. The first proof-of-concept applications were limited, as we are in the process of implementing fast evaluation of second-order derivatives (see the Evaluation of Determinants project).

Fractional occupation numbers

To better understand the RPA properties, a study of calculations with fractional occupation numbers has been initiated. This type of analysis allows one to estimate how a method handles the N-electrons self-interaction error, or, more accurately, the delocalization error. Some early implementation in the MOLPRO quantum chemistry package yields interesting results useful to compare the behavior of different RPA approximations.

OEP Double-hybrid

Together with the project on fractional occupation number calculations (details), we became interested in the derivation of an orbital-optimized double-hybrid scheme, making use of the optimized-effective-potential (OEP) method. In this scheme, the orbitals are optimized using a local potential corresponding to the complete exchange-correlation energy expression that includes the second-order Möller-Plesset (MP2) correlation contribution. This leads to an original novel one-parameter OEP-based self-consistent double-hybrid functional. The OEP-based self-consistency improves the accuracy of electron affinities, restores the meaning of the LUMO orbital energy as being connected to a neutral excitation energy and provides reasonably accurate exchange-correlation potentials and correlated densities.

High-harmonic generation spectra

We explored the computation of high-harmonic generation spectra in atoms by means of Gaussian basis sets in time-dependent close coupling approaches. We investigated the efficiency of Gaussian functions specifically designed for the description of the continuum proposed by Kaufmann et al. ; we notably studied the effect of increasing the basis set cardinal number, the number of diffuse basis functions, and the number of Gaussian pseudo-continuum basis functions for various laser parameters. Our results show that the latter significantly improve the description of the low-lying continuum states, and provide a satisfactory agreement with grid calculations.

RPA formalisms

I worked on the different formalisms in which the RPA equations can be derived. I in particular explored in a systematic and new way the formalism called “dielectric matrix formalism”. The examination and comparison of the different RPA formalisms reveals the underlying unity between them and allows to obtain crucial information about the nature of the approximations that are made during the a priori very different derivations. I implemented this dielectric matrix formalism and the various approximations that can be made in this context in the MOLPRO quantum chemistry package, including a new Clenshaw-Curtis quadrature for the integration over the frequency dimension.

pOO: Local orbitals

The use of local orbitals is one of the path that can be taken to reduce the computational cost of calculations. I studied the virtual localized orbitals that are the “projected oscillatory orbitals” (pOO), an original idea of Boys that has not been developped in the literature. The basic equations concerning the pOO have been derived during my thesis, including multipolar expansions of the bi-electronic integrals in the basis of the pOO ; it has also been shown that the matrix elements of the dipolar moment between an occupied localized orbital and a pOO virtual localized orbital reduces to the expression of the overlap between two pOOs. This non-trivial result vastly simplifies the RPA equations that can be written in the pOO basis.

Spin-unrestricted generalisation

To widen the field of applications of our RPA methods, the derivations of most of the formalisms have been generalized to open shell systems and this has been implemented in the MOLPRO quantum chemistry package. Extended tests on datasets of atomisation energies (AE49) and of barrier heights energies (BH24) showed the excellent performance of a version of RPA with exchange called “SO2” when it is used in a range separation context. This variant was previously already known to have good performances on the S22 dataset, but this kind of generalizations and extensions of application field allows to better grasp the roots of the relative qualities of the different versions of RPA.

Basis convergence

Among the advantages of the use of range separation methods, one expects the convergence of the calculations with respect to the size of the gaussian basis set to be faster since the long-range wavefunction is freed from the treatment of the electron-electron coalescence. Indeed, we showed, on the one hand, the exponential convergence of the quality of the expansion called “partial-wave” of the long-range wavefunction at the electron-electron coalescence with respect to the angular momentum of the expansion and, on the other hand, the also exponential convergence of the error made on the total RSH+MP2 energy with respect to the cardinal number of the Dunning basis. Based on these observations, we proposed an extrapolation method for the total energy of range-separated calculations.

Direct space

I have worked on the response functions (found when doing RPA calculations), considering explicitly their dependance to the real space variables. In my thesis I created tools to calculate these functions on parallelepipedic real space grids, and on so-called “DFT-type” grids. A long-term goal could be the generation of grids such as to better sample the space between the atoms.

Gradients of the RPA correlation energy

I derived the equations allowing to compute the analytical gradients of the RPA correlation energies in the context of range separation. The formalism that was developped, which uses the lagrangian technique, allows an all-in-one derivation of the short- and long-range terms that emerge in the expressions of the gradient. These terms show interesting parallels. The equations were implemented in the MOLPRO quantum chemistry package, and this now allows to perform geometry optimisation at the RSH+RPA levels, and to obtain dipoles calculated at the RSH+RPA levels.

Selected excitations in localized orbitals

Working with localized orbitals, we were able to show that long-range correlation contributions to range-separated density-functional theory can be computed with reduced computational effort. Indeed, the “dispersion-only” approximation to the dimer captures most of the interaction energy of the dimer (this avoids calculation of the counterpoise-corrected monomer).

Software Contributions

Molpro

Molpro is a comprehensive system of ab initio programs for advanced molecular electronic structure calculations. It comprises efficient and well parallelized programs for standard computational chemistry applications, such as DFT with a large choice of functionals, as well as state-of-the art high-level coupled-cluster and multi-reference wave function methods. Molpro is very easy to use for standard applications, but also includes many advanced options for expert applications.

(from http://www.molpro.net)

I have first implemented functionnalities in the MOLPRO quantum chemistry package at the beginning of my PhD thesis, from 2010 to 2013, but mainly I have been in charge of its management and support during my post-doc at the Laboratoire de Chimie Théorique at the Université Pierre et Marie Curie, from 2014 to 2016.

I unified different pre-existing RPA developments and improved the performance of the code (memory management, CPU time of the transformation of bi-electronic integrals, etc). I also implemented all the tools necessary to the many projects underwent in that time, such as the range-separated analytical gradients (details), the generalization to open-shell systems (details) and the calculations using fractional occupation numbers (details).

Dice

Dice is a program principally written by Sandeep Sharma that implements the semistochastic heat bath configuration interaction (SHCI) algorithm for ab initio Hamiltonians of quantum chemical systems.

Unlike full configuration interaction (FCI), SHCI can be used to treat active spaces containing 30 to 100 orbitals. SHCI is able to accomplish this by taking advantage of the fact that although the full Hilbert space may be enormous, only a small fraction of the determinants in the space have appreciable coefficients.

(from https://sanshar.github.io/Dice)

In the Dice program, I am involved in the implementation of perturbative corrections (notably MRLCC, see details), of the self-consistent field (HCISCF) feature (details) and of the extension to the treatment of relativistic effects (details).

PySCF

PySCF is an ab initio computational chemistry program implemented in Python program language. The package aims to provide a simple, light-weight and efficient platform for electronic structure theory developing and simulation.

Although most functions are written in Python, the computation critical modules are intensively optimized in C. The package works as efficient as other C/Fortran based quantum chemistry program.

(from https://github.com/sunqm/pyscf)

I implemented some simple modifications to the Block and Dice interfaces with PySCF, notably for the calculation of fully internally contracted NEVPT and MRLCC (details).

CHAMP

CHAMP is a quantum Monte Carlo suite of programs for electronic structure calculations principally written by Cyrus Umrigar and Claudia Filippi with major contributions by Julien Toulouse and Devrim Guclu, postdocs at Cornell and smaller contributions by others.

The following three basic capabilities define the package: VMC and DMC, optimization of many-body wave functions by variance minimization (FIT) and by any linear combination of energy and variance minimization (VMC).

(from http://pages.physics.cornell.edu/~cyrus/champ.html)

In CHAMP, I implemented the necessary tools to calculate time-dependent linear-response properties in VMC (details), and I am involved in the implementation of very fast and efficient calculations of determinants and second-order derivatives (details).

    Abstract

    In this thesis are shown developments in the random phase approximation (RPA) in the context of range-separated theories. We present advances in the formalism of the RPA in general, and particularly in the "dielectric matrix" formulation of RPA, which is explored in details. We show a summary of a work on the RPA equations with localised orbitals, especially developments of the virtual localized orbitals that are the "projected oscillatory orbitals" (pOO). A program has been written to calculate functions such as the exchange hole, the response function, etc on real space grid (parallelepipedic or of the "DFT" type) ; some of those visualisations are shown here. In the real space, we offer an adaptation of the effective energy denominator approximation (EED), originally developped in the reciprocal space in solid physics. The analytical gradients of the RPA correlation energies in the context of range separation has been derived. The formalism developped here with a lagrangian allows an all-in-one derivation of the short- and long-range terms that emerge in the expressions of the gradient. These terms show interesting parallels. Geometry optimisations at the RSH+dRPA-I and RSH+SOSEX levels on a set of 16 molecules are shown, as well as calculations and visualisations of correlated densities at the RSH+dRPA-I level.
